Intro

Tian'anmen was built in the 15th year of Yongle of the Ming Dynasty (1417). It was originally named "Chengtianmen", which means "Chengtianmen set off from heaven and receive orders from heaven". It was renamed Tian 'anmen in 1651, the eighth year of Shunzhi in the Qing Dynasty. Composed of Chengtai and Chenglou two parts, there is white marble Xumi seat, a total height of 34.7 meters.

Yonghe Palace, where Emperor Qianlong was born, produced two emperors and became the "Dragon Hiding place of happiness", so the temple was of yellow tiles and red walls, the same size as the imperial palace in the Forbidden City. In 1744, the Lama Temple was converted into a lama temple and appointed the Minister of the Prime Minister's Affairs, the King, to manage its affairs. It became the center of the Qing government in charge of the national Tibetan Buddhist affairs. Lama Temple was the highest Buddhist temple in China in the middle and late Qing Dynasty.

Intro

The Summer Palace, an imperial garden of the Qing Dynasty, covers an area of 3.009 square kilometers (including 2.97 square kilometers of the Summer Palace World Cultural Heritage area), about three-quarters of which is water. Adjacent to the Old Summer Palace. It is a large landscape garden built on the base of Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill, modeled on Hangzhou West Lake and drawing on the design techniques of Jiangnan gardens. It is also the most complete preserved royal palace and garden, known as the "Museum of Royal Gardens".

Intro

The Old Summer Palace first built in 1707, consists of the Old Summer Palace, Changchun Palace and Qichun Palace. It covers an area of 350 hectares, of which 140 hectares are on the water surface. There are more than 100 garden landscapes and the building area is more than 160,000 square meters. It is a large royal palace founded and operated by the Qing Dynasty emperors in more than 150 years.
