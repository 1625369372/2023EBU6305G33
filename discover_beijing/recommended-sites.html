Recommended Sites

Site 1

The Palace Museum

The Palace Museum in Beijing is a comprehensive museum established on October 10, 1925. It is a large comprehensive museum of ancient art established on the basis of the Ming and Qing imperial palaces and their collections. It is also the largest museum of ancient Chinese culture and art.

Site 2

Universal Beijing Resort

Beijing Global Resort is the third in Asia and the fifth Universal Studios theme park in the world, including Harry Potter's Magic World, Transformers Base, Kung Fu Panda, Hollywood, Future Water World, Miners Park, Jurassic World Nubra Island and Beijing Universal City Avenue. District. Since the opening of the park, it has attracted countless tourists.

Site 3

National Stadium-Bird's Nest

The National Stadium (Bird's Nest), located in the south of the central area of the Beijing Olympic Park, is the main stadium of the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games. It held the opening and closing ceremonies of the Olympic Games, Paralympic Games, track and field competitions and football finals. After the Olympic Games, it has become a large-scale professional place for Beijing citizens to participaten sports activities and enjoy sports entertainment, and has become a landmark sports building and Olympic heritage.

Site 4

Beijing Zoo

Beijing Zoo is located in Xizhimenwai Street, Xicheng District, Beijing, China. It is the earliest zoo in China and has the largest variety of animals on display. More than 5,000 animals of more than 500 species are raised in the exhibition; more than 500 species of marine fish and marine life are more than 10,000 tails. It receives more than 6 million Chinese and foreign tourists every year. It is one of the largest zoos in China and a world-renowned zoo.

Site 5

Summer Palace

The Summer Palace, the royal garden of the Qing Dynasty in China, formerly known as the Qingyi Garden, is located in the western suburbs of Beijing, adjacent to the Yuanmingyuan. It is a large landscape garden based on Kunming Lake and Wanshou Mountain, based on the West Lake of Hangzhou, and draws on the design techniques of the Jiangnan Garden. It is also the most complete royal palace and is known as the "Royal Garden Museum".

Site 6

Capital Museumt

As a large-scale comprehensive museum affiliated to the Beijing Municipal Government, the Capital Museum has rich connotations and perfect functions. It preserves and displays the precious relics of about 700,000 years of Beijing's human settlements, more than 3,000 years of urban history, and more than 800 years of Kyoto history. It also collects and displays many treasures of Chinese civilization, with a total of more than 200,000.
